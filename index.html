2010-2011 Season

Head Coach, Bill Snyder, led the 2010 team to an improved 7-6 record, with a 3-5 record in conference play which was good for third in the North division. The season ended with a loss to Syracuse in the 2010 Pinstripe Bowl. This was K-State's first bowl appearance since Ron Prince lead the school to the 2006 Texas Bowl.

2011-2012 Season

In 2011, Coach Snyder led the team to a 10-3 record, a second-place finish in the Big 12 Conference, and a No. 15 ranking in the final AP Poll. The team finished the season with a loss to the No. 7 Arkansas Razorbacks in the Cotton Bowl. It was the first 10-win season and first top-20 ranking for Kansas State since the 2003 season. After the season Snyder was named the Woody Hayes Coach of the Year and the Sporting News National Coach of the Year, as well as the Big 12 Conference Coach of the Year.

2012-2013 Season

In 2012, Snyder's Wildcats won the Big 12 Conference. K-State last won the Big 12 in 2003. Kansas State also earned the school's first No. 1 ranking in the BCS standings after starting the season 10-0, before falling to Baylor in its 11th game of the season. The Wildcats earned the conference's automatic berth in the 2013 Fiesta Bowl, where the team lost to the No. 5 Oregon Ducks, 35-17. After the season, Coach Snyder was named the conference coach of the year for the seventh time in his career. He also was awarded the Bobby Dodd Coach of the Year Award for the second time.

2013-2014 Season

In 2013, after completing the regular season with a 7-5 record, the Kansas State Wildcats returned to a bowl game for the fourth straight year. They were selected to play in the 2013 Buffalo Wild Wings Bowl and played the Michigan Wolverines. Winning this game, Kansas State snapped a five-game bowl losing streak, beating Michigan in the Buffalo Wild Wings Bowl, 31-14.

2014-2015 Season

In 2014, the Wildcats were led to a 9-3 record by Snyder with losses to Auburn of the SEC, Baylor and TCU, finishing third in the Big 12. The Wildcats had been ranked in all polls with the highest ranking in the new College Football Playoff rankings at number 7. Kansas State became bowl eligible after winning its sixth game against Texas on October 25. The No. 11 Wildcats were invited to the Alamo Bowl where they fell to No. 14 UCLA in San Antonio, Texas, 40-35.

2015-2016 Season

2015 was the 120th season in school history. They finished the season 6-7 overall and 3-6 in Big 12 play to finish in eighth place. K-State football rattled off 3 straight wins against South Dakota, UTSA, and Louisiana Tech. K-State then lost their next 5 games with their most notable loss coming from a 0-55 showing at Oklahoma. After dropping 5 straight, K-State won their last 3 games to become bowl eligible. The season was doomed from the start as starting Quarterback, Sophomore Jesse Ertz, hurt his knee in the opening game of the season and was ruled out for the season. The Quarteback position was shared between backup Joe Hubener and Senior Wide Reciever, Kody Cook. They were invited to the Liberty Bowl where they lost to Arkansas 23-45.

2016-2017 Season

The Wildcats were led by head coach Bill Snyder in his 25th overall and eighth straight season since taking over for his second tenure in 2009. Coming off a lost Sophomore season, Jesse Ertz won back the starting Quarteback role and played in all 13 games. Ertz let the Wildcats in the air and on the ground with a team leading 1,012 rushing yards. It was a shared backfield of Charles Jones, Justin Silmon, and Alex Barnes. K-Sate opened up their 2016 campaign with a loss to Stanford in Palo Alto. K-State finished the year with a 9-4 record and a bid to the Texas Bowl where they defeated former Big 12 foe, Texas A&M.

2017-2018 Season

in January 2017 it was reported by many Kansas media outlets that Bill Snyder has been traveling to Houston, Texas for treatment for a "serious life-threatening ailment". On February 12, 2017 Snyder announced that he has been diagnosed with throat cancer and had been receiving treatment in Manhattan, as well at University of Kansas Medical Center, and in Houston, Texas. The starting Quarterback role was shared between Jesse Ertz, Alex Delton, and Skylar Thompson. Senior Alex Barnes led the Wildcats in rushing yards. K-State's loss in Nashville against SEC bottom-feeder Vanderbilt sent doubt into the KSU fanbase as the season went on. With an average season at best and a solid road win against #10 Okalahom State towards the end of the season, K-State finsished the season with a 8-5 record and defeated the UCLA Bruin in the Cactus Bowl.

2018-2019 Season

The 2018 season started with a rocky start. K-State opened the season at home with a nail-biting win against FCS opponent, South Dakota. Losing 12-24 going into the 4th quarter, the Wildcats were saved by a 85 yard punt return by Isaiah Zuber and a 10 yard touchdown pass to Zuber from Quarterback Skylar Thompson. The Coyotes could of sent the game into over-time but the field goal was missed by their kicker. The 'Cats finished the season with a 5-7 record and missed bowl season for the first time since the 2010 season. On December 2 Snyder, who had signed a five-year extension before the season began, announced his retirement. His final record was 215-117-1 in 27 seasons. On December 10, North Dakota State head coach Chris Klieman was hired as Snyder's successor.

2019-2020 Season

The Wildcats were led by first-year head coach Chris Klieman, who accepted the role after the retirement of long-time head coach Bill Snyder. KSU opened up the season with a blow-out win against Nichols State, a 52-0 no-doubter against Bowling Green, and an impressive road win against Mississippi State whom the Wildcats lost to in Bill Snyder Family Stadium the previous season. K-State shocked #5 Oklahoma in Manhattan and reached #20 in the polls during the season. K-State finished the season with an 8-5 record and a bowl trip to the Liberty Bowl where they lost to Navy 17-20 on a trick play.
